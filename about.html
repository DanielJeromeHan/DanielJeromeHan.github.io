About

Why We Took Action: The Deidre Lew Service Award The Deidre Lew Service Award was created by the Tanglin Trust School (TTS) Foundation in 2018, as a way for TTS students and alumni to give back to the community. It comes with funds from the Foundation in hopes of supporting and driving forward student-led initiatives. We applied for the Deirdre Lew Service Award with the aim of raising awareness about the importance of marine conservation and coral reefs. Our mission stemmed from the UN sustainable goal #14 ‘Life Below Water’, and we hope to establish a long term project, a legacy, by taking the initial steps and inspiring the students of Tanglin to continue coral conservation efforts at Rawa for years to come. Our team is made up of Tanglin students ranging from the age of 12 to 17, all of whom bring unique ideas and initiative to the table. We believe this diversity makes our project more inclusive and accessible to all students that are eager to learn and help. We look forward to the day, when members of the Tanglin family visit the island, they will be able to see the enduring legacy we have left behind. We strive to be brave adventurers, strong-minded people, leaders and action-takers. The voice for the oceans.
